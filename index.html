Game of Life:

Game of Life was my first big project (Made in C++ and wxWidgets). The Game of Life is a concept created by the mathematician John Conway. Rules: Any living cell in the current universe with fewer than 2 living neighbors dies in the next generation due to under-population. If a cell meets this criterion in the universe array, it is marked as dead. Any living cell with more than 3 living neighbors will die in the next generation due to over-population. If this condition exists in the universe, the cell is killed. Any living cell with 2 or 3 living neighbors will survive into the next generation. If this condition exists in the universe, the cell remains alive. Any dead cell with exactly 3 living neighbors will be born into the next generation due to reproduction. If this condition exists in the universe, that cell is made alive. My biggest obstacle with this project was implementing the rules, which took me far too long to get correct. By the time I finally got them right, I had fallen pretty far behind. However, I learned something quite valuable: no matter how difficult things get, you should not quit until the whistle has blown.

Movie Search Application:

Next, I chose to create a movie search app in TypeScript and React. This application fetches movie information and images from APIs. You can explore the most popular movies, new releases, browse by various genres, perform searches, and use advanced search features. Clicking on a movie displays its card containing all relevant information. My biggest challenges with this project were learning a new language, building a website, and implementing advanced search functionality within a month. I mainly overcame these challenges by watching many YouTube tutorials. However, I was filled with euphoria once it was complete. What I learned is that I am capable of learning and adapting to new concepts rather quickly if I put my mind to it.
